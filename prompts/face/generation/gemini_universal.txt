The Universal Prompt For Gemini 3.1 Pro (June 2026):
Universal Female ID-Preserving Portrait Prompt (Enhanced Fidelity)
I. Core Identity and Technical Standardization
Subject: A hyper-realistic, high-resolution 2D portrait photograph of a female, tightly framed from head to shoulders, ensuring the full head structure, including all
hairstyle components, is visible, even if the reference image is cropped at the top or sides.
Goal: This output must be a rigid, biometric-preserving digital twin of the face and full head structure provided in the input image. Use high-fidelity, logical in-
painting to complete and display the entire head and hairstyle seamlessly.
Pose: Strictly frontal, perfectly symmetrical. The head is centered with zero tilt. The eyes are at the same level and directed straight at the camera. Lips are aligned;
the chin is vertically straight.
Expression: Calm and strictly neutral (no smile, no frown).
Lighting: Even, diffused, shadowless studio lighting (as if from softboxes), ensuring all facial contours and textures are equally visible without harsh specular
highlights or shadows under the nose or neck.
Background: Clean, seamless, pure white ( #ffffff ).
Artifact Removal: Remove all digital grain, compression artifacts (JPEG blocking), and correct color casts (White Balance normalization) based on a gray-world
assumption.
II. Specific Feature Mapping (Placeholder Directives)
The AI must map the following features from the input image with high fidelity:
[EYES]: Preserve the exact unique eye shape, size, inter-ocular distance, and precise iris color/texture seen in the input image. Crucially, match the specific color, not
just a generic type (e.g., if a deep amber, preserve deep amber, do not drift to generic light hazel). Zero tolerance for color shift.
[SKIN]: Retain the natural, unadorned skin tone profile and skin texture. Digital smoothing is forbidden. Preserve only the unique aesthetic markers present in the
reference. CRITICAL: Do not add pores, freckles, or moles if they are not present in the reference. Texture fidelity must be a direct match.
[FACIAL TOPOLOGY]: Maintain the precise geometrical ratios and distances between facial landmarks (Eye-to-Nose, Nose-to-Mouth, Mouth-to-Chin).
[MAKEUP]: Analyze the type, color, and placement of the makeup in the reference. If the subject has natural skin, the output must have natural skin. If the subject is
wearing a specific color of lipstick or defined eyeliner, replicate that makeup style and color with high fidelity, but ensure it looks refined, seamlessly blended, and
adapted to appear natural and integrated with the skin texture. The system must prioritize making the application look smooth and natural, not heavy, caked, or
artificially distinct layers, regardless of the perceived application style in the reference.
[HAIR]: Replicate the hair type, texture, and color from the reference with perfect accuracy. The styling should be polished to frame the face and head neatly,
removing random flyaways or stray strands. Critically, if the reference image is cropped such that a hairstyle (like a bun or crown) is cut off, the AI must logically
and seamlessly in-paint and complete the entire hairstyle structure, ensuring the final output shows the full head and hairstyle without cropping at the top.
III. Acceptance & Rejection Scope (Safety Controls)
ACCEPTANCE SCOPE (Authorized to Modify or Replicate):
Polishing: Standard studio retouching is authorized to remove temporary blemishes (pimples) or smooth messy hair strands if they do not affect key identity
markers.
Symmetrization: Minor pose corrections to bring a slight angle (e.g., 5-degree tilt) into a perfect 0-degree frontal symmetry are authorized, provided facial
landmarks are not distorted.
Comprehensive Framing: Authorizes logical in-painting to complete any cropped features (especially cropped-out hair buns, crowns, and heads) to ensure a
complete head-and-shoulders frame.
Refined Makeup Application: Authorizes making the application of makeup look seamless and refined while strictly keeping the original color and style.
REJECTION SCOPE (Actions to Avoid or Triggers to Reject Input):
Strict Occlusion (Reject): Reject any input image where more than 15% of the primary facial landmarks (eyes, nose, mouth) are obscured by hair, hands, or other
objects.
Angle Limitation (Reject): Reject any input image where the pose is more than a 3/4 view. If the face is not significantly frontal, do not generate.
Accessory Removal (Avoid/Reject): Do not generate eyewear, headwear, large piercings, or heavy jewelry (except small, standard earrings), even if they are in the
input image. If these items are key identifiers (e.g., a religious head covering that must be kept), the user must explicitly instruct retention, overriding this clause.
Gender (Check): Verify that the input image is a female face.
Resolution (Reject): Reject any input image with a native resolution below 512x512 pixels.
Drifting Details (Reject): Reject any output that generates details (pores, moles, freckles, new eye color) not found in the reference, or fails to in-paint a missing head
structure. Run a "Sanity Check" iterative, recursive feedback loop comparison against the reference to ensure identity integrity.
